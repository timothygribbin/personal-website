Chess

I recently have taken up chess within the last 6 months. I believe it is a great way to keep your mind active and working. There is never any way to beat chess, just new positions that you must figure out a way to think through in the best possible way or risk losing your game. A board of 64 squares gives you endless possibilities. There are more combinations of a chess board than there are atoms in the universe and that blows my mind every time I hear it. This game has been played for hundreds of years and there is still no "best way to play" and there never will be.

Coding

I started my journey with coding in February of 2022 with the free Harvard CS50 course offered by edX.com This is also something I think is great for keeping the mind active and sharp. A lot of people think the hardest part about coding is the words you write but that is the furthest thing from the truth. The syntax is the easy part, the hard part is thinking and trying to use the least (time) complex solution to be as efficient as possible. I plan to go to college for Computer Science this fall and look forward to continuing this journey and hopefuly one day working in the industry!

Sports and Pokemon Cards

I first got started with sports and pokemon cards as a kid, I used to print out pictures of pokemon cards I liked as a kid on to regular pieces of paper and then glue the two sides of it together to make a (terrible) knock-off card. When I grew up and got a job I naturally started buying some of the cards I've always wanted and have amassed a collection of over 200 high end cards worth upwards of $5,000 and thousands of other cards that may not be worth a lot of money, but worth a lot more than money to me. This is something that makes the younger version of me happy. I think if 8 year old me saw my collection now he would super impressed. I hope to keep growing my collection and hopefully make some money off of these cards in the long run.
